A few words about us...

We are a research group in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington in Seattle. Our mission is to uncover how technology can be biased against people around the globe who are unlike the small groups of people that created it -- and to build systems that help designers, developers, and researchers better support the needs and perspectives of different people. Check out our virtual lab, the LabintheWild, or other ongoing projects below!

Studying People Around the World With LabintheWild

Our virtual lab has been running since 2012 and has been visited by more than 5 million volunteer participants.

Culture Clash in Conversational AI

Can communicating with conversational AI have effects similar to culture clash?

Anticipating Unintended Consequences

How can we help developers and designers foresee potential adverse effects of digital technology on society?

Cultural Mismatches in Technology

We study how the design of technology promotes norms and values that may be misaligned with those of users.

Tools for Detecting Bias in Website Designs

We have developed tools that predict visual appeal, trustworthiness, and color differentiability.

Bias Due to Language Style

From security interfaces to online experiments, we've found that language formality and complexity affects users in surprising ways.

Communicating Science to Diverse People

Much of our work involves citizen scientists. How can we communicate scientific findings to them?

Studying Less WEIRD People

Research findings are mostly based on participants who are Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and Democratic. How can we change this?

Language models and their WEIRDness

We have found that NLP datasets and models often are often biased towards Western and college-educated views.
